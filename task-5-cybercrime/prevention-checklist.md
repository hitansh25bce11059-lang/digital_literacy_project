# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (India)

## ✅ General Online Safety

- Use **strong, unique passwords** for all accounts (avoid using the same password everywhere)  
- Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** on email, social media, and banking apps  
- Do not click on **unknown links** received via SMS, email, or social media  
- Verify the sender before downloading **attachments or files**  
- Avoid using **public Wi-Fi** for sensitive activities like banking or logging into accounts  
- Keep your **software and apps updated** to prevent security vulnerabilities  
- Limit the personal information you share on **social media platforms**  
- Block and report **suspicious accounts or messages** immediately

## 🧠 Reflection (100–150 words)

While researching cybercrime and online safety, what surprised me most was how easily people fall for simple scams like phishing links and fake UPI requests. Many attacks do not require advanced hacking skills — they rely on human mistakes, urgency, or lack of awareness. This made me realize that anyone can become a target, especially students who frequently use digital platforms for payments and communication.

One habit I will personally change is being more cautious before clicking on links or approving any online transaction. I will always verify the source, double-check payment details, and avoid acting in haste. Staying alert and informed is the most effective way to prevent cyber fraud.
